"Shraddha aur Saburi" — Faith and Patience

Shirdi Sai Baba was a spiritual master and saint who lived in Shirdi, Maharashtra, India. Revered by millions across faiths — Hinduism and Islam alike — he taught the unity of God, love for all beings, and the power of devoted surrender.

✦ ✦ ✦

About Sai Baba

🕌🛕

Universal Saint

Sai Baba transcended religious boundaries. He lived in a mosque he called Dwarkamai yet performed Hindu rites. He was a living symbol that all paths lead to the one God.

❤️

Love for Humanity

He spent his life feeding the poor, healing the sick, and comforting the troubled. His door was open to every person regardless of caste, creed, or social standing.

🌟

Divine Presence

Devotees across generations have experienced his living grace — through answered prayers, timely guidance, and miraculous interventions that continue to this day.

📿

Eternal Guru

He initiated disciples without distinction, bestowing spiritual knowledge through simple acts, parables, and the silence of his presence. He is considered a Sadguru — a true spiritual guide.

Life & Timeline

~1838

Birth

Believed to have been born around 1838, exact birthplace and parentage remain a mystery — as Baba himself kept it so.

~1858

Arrives in Shirdi

A young ascetic arrives in Shirdi village, Maharashtra, and takes up residence under a neem tree.

1858–

Dwarkamai

Baba makes an old dilapidated mosque his permanent home, lighting a sacred dhuni (fire) that he kept burning continuously.

1886

Maha Samadhi Experience

Baba appeared to stop breathing for three days, then revived — an event seen as a divine revelation of his mastery over life and death.

1910s

Growing Devotion

Pilgrims, scholars, and seekers from across India begin flocking to Shirdi. His fame spreads throughout Maharashtra and beyond.

1918

Maha Samadhi

On 15 October 1918, Baba took Maha Samadhi (conscious departure from the body) at Dwarkamai. His tomb in Shirdi remains a living place of grace.

Divine Miracles

🔥

The Sacred Dhuni

Baba kept a sacred fire (dhuni) burning at Dwarkamai. The ash (Udi) from this fire is said to carry healing properties and is distributed to devotees to this day.

💧

Water into Oil

When shopkeepers refused to give oil for lamps, Baba filled the lamps with water — and they burned brightly throughout the night, witnessed by the entire village.

🌿

Healing the Sick

Countless devotees report miraculous recoveries from illness after receiving Baba's Udi, his blessings, or simply by surrendering to his grace in prayer.

👁️

Omniscience

Baba repeatedly demonstrated knowledge of events happening far away, revealing the thoughts and deeds of people he had never met — affirming his divine awareness.

Visit Shirdi

📍

Location

Shirdi, Ahmednagar District, Maharashtra, India — approx. 300 km from Mumbai and 130 km from Pune.

🕰️

Darshan Timings

The Samadhi Mandir is open from 4:00 AM to 11:00 PM. Kakad Aarti at 5:15 AM, Shej Aarti at 10:30 PM.

🚂

How to Reach

Nearest railway station: Sainagar Shirdi (dedicated station). Nearest airport: Shirdi Airport, with flights from major Indian cities.

🏨

Accommodation

The Shri Saibaba Sansthan Trust provides affordable lodging for pilgrims. Many private hotels are also available near the temple.

🍛

Prasadalaya

Free meals (prasad) are served to thousands of pilgrims daily at the Trust's Prasadalaya — a tradition Baba started during his lifetime.

📞

Contact

Shri Saibaba Sansthan Trust, Shirdi — 423 109, Maharashtra. Official website: sai.org.in
